## Why this came up

The `cdsco-reviewer-tool` repo contains an XGBoost + TF-IDF classifier deployed on Modal at `services/sae-classifier/`. Surfaced during Sprint 1 work. Question raised: could we port this XGBoost pattern to ClearPath's CDSCO classification path to cut LLM cost?

Answer: no. The model solves a different problem and its accuracy ceiling sits well below ClearPath's bar. Below: the audit that produced that conclusion.

## What the SAE classifier actually is

Audited the code at `cdsco-reviewer-tool/services/sae-classifier/` and `modal-services/sae-classifier/` on 2026-05-07. Facts:

- **Problem:** severity classification of post-market adverse-event narratives. Output: `death | disability | hospitalization | other`. Closed-set, 4 classes.
- **NOT a CDSCO regulatory classifier.** Does not predict A/B/C/D class, IMDRF category, or regulatory pathway. Different problem entirely.
- **Training data:** 180 synthetic Indian SAE narratives generated by Claude Opus 4.7 (`experiments/generate_sae.py`). Labels are **self-labeled by Opus at generation time** — Opus writes a narrative AND assigns its own severity_label. No FDA crosswalk. No human labeling.
- **Accuracy:** macro-F1 0.681 on held-out 20% of the same Opus-generated corpus. MCC 0.587. Target was >0.65, so it passes the in-distribution bar.
- **Real-world expected accuracy:** estimated 0.55-0.65, since the train-test distribution match (Opus → Opus) overstates true generalization.
- **Inference path:** TF-IDF (max_features=5000, ngram=(1,2)) → XGBoost (max_depth=6, n_estimators=100) → top class + softmax + `fallback_recommended` flag (when confidence < 0.60). FastAPI single endpoint. Wrapped on Modal.
- **Production scale:** ~$2/month at current SAE volume. Latency 1.5-1.8s warm.

## Why this doesn't transfer to ClearPath classification

1. **Different output space.** SAE: 4 closed severity classes. ClearPath: A/B/C/D × IMDRF category × regulatory pathway × gap analysis × narrative rationale. Structured + reasoned + multi-field. A 4-class flat-softmax classifier doesn't address this shape.

2. **Accuracy ceiling below ClearPath's bar.** SAE in-distribution F1 of 0.681 implies real-world ~0.55-0.65. ClearPath's eval bar is ≥90% on cdsco_class. 25-35 point gap.

3. **Training labels are LLM-self-labeled.** Distilling an LLM into XGBoost gives you cost savings AND inherits the LLM's classification biases — you can't catch them with a holdout drawn from the same LLM's output. Quality ceiling is at-best whatever LLM produced the labels. The SAE classifier is essentially an Opus-mimic with TF-IDF features.

4. **Cost savings don't justify regression.** SAE classifier costs ~$2/month at its current volume. ClearPath synth on Opus 4.7 costs ~$0.118/call. At eval-validated free-tier volume, the absolute saving from porting would be <$10/month per 100 assessments. Not worth a 25-35 point accuracy hit on the regulatory pathway determination founders pay ₹4,999+ for.

## What the SAE classifier IS for

**Sprint 6+ post-market surveillance features.** The classifier is exactly the right tool for:

- **PSUR (Periodic Safety Update Report) generation** — ingests reported adverse events, classifies severity, surfaces the high-severity tail for narrative summarisation.
- **MvPI (Materiovigilance Programme of India) adverse event triage** — same pattern; classify incoming SAE narratives, route disability/death cases to manual review with the fallback flag for confidence < 0.60.
- **Post-market trending dashboards** — aggregate severity distribution per device family over time.

For these, 0.681 macro-F1 is acceptable because:
- The fallback flag routes uncertain cases to humans (0.60 threshold is reviewer-tunable)
- The 4-class severity space is closed and well-defined
- Severity tagging is post-event surveillance, not pre-market regulatory determination

**Backlog re-positioning:** treat the SAE classifier as "PSUR/MvPI supporting infrastructure," not "future classification speedup."

## When to revisit hybrid LLM-verdict + classical-ML-confidence

The hybrid pattern (LLM produces verdict + ML produces calibrated confidence over the verdict) stays valid as a future architecture, but ONLY when triggered by validated business signals.

**Required pre-conditions (ALL must hold):**
- ≥500 QC-reviewed CDSCO classification cases (real customer data, not synthetic)
- ≥3 months of QC review flowing continuously (i.e. sustainable label production)

**Plus AT LEAST ONE of:**
- QC correction rate >15% on LLM verdicts (LLM is wrong often enough that confidence calibration adds business value)
- Concrete partner demand for explicit confidence scores (incubator/regulator wants probability per case)
- Volume >100K assessments/month (cost regime shifts; LLM-only economics start to bite)

**Anti-trigger conditions (ANY of these means defer):**
- QC correction rate <5% (LLM is accurate enough; confidence calibration adds noise, not signal)
- No partner has asked for confidence scores
- No cost pressure (per-assessment cost stays a small fraction of revenue)

**If triggered, the build:**
- Fresh XGBoost (or comparable) on the QC-reviewed CDSCO labels — **NOT a port of the SAE classifier** (wrong label space, wrong distribution)
- LLM produces verdict + rationale (unchanged)
- Classical model produces calibrated confidence over the verdict
- Disagreement above threshold → flag for QC review

**Earliest realistic timeline:** Month 9-12 / Sprint 8-10.
