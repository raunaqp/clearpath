## Q1 — Is ₹25K too low for Tier 3 Submission Concierge?

**Short answer: Yes, it's underpriced for what the name implies.**

### Market comparables

| Provider | Service level | Typical fee |
|---|---|---|
| Operon Strategist | Full CDSCO submission support | ₹3–10L |
| CliniExperts | MD-12 + MD-9 end-to-end | ₹2–8L |
| Asia Actual India | Class C/D submission | ₹5–15L |
| Maverick Partners | Regulatory + QMS package | ₹1–5L |
| Freelance regulatory consultants | Document review + 1 iteration | ₹50K–2L |
| **ClearPath Tier 3 (proposed)** | **Review + refinement + 1 iteration** | **₹25K** |

### Unit economics at ₹25K

Expert reviewer time per case: 10–20 hours at ₹1,500–3,000/hour = **₹15K–60K in expert cost alone**. On heavy cases you're breakeven or underwater. There's no room for platform margin, customer support, or any iteration beyond the promised one.

### Three options

**Option A — Keep ₹25K but tighten the scope.** Rename to "Readiness Audit & Expert Review." Strip back the "submission support" promise. Position as: *"An expert's second opinion on your draft pack, not a full submission service."* Clean value prop. Defensible economics.

**Option B — Raise to ₹50K with current scope.** Covers expert time + thin margin. "Submission Concierge" label holds up. Still massive undercut vs consultants (₹3–10L market). My top recommendation.

**Option C — Split into Tier 3a (₹25K) and Tier 3b (₹1L).**
- Tier 3a — "Expert Review Pack" (₹25K): document review + classification validation + 1 iteration.
- Tier 3b — "Submission Concierge" (₹1L): 3a + QMS setup + clinical validation plan + 2 iterations + submission support.

**My strong recommendation: Option B (raise to ₹50K).** Here's why:
- ₹50K is still massively below market (₹3L–10L for comparables)
- Covers honest unit economics without cannibalising Tier 1
- ₹50K is still in "founder can authorise without a board meeting" zone
- "Submission Concierge" label holds up and doesn't over-promise
- You can introduce Tier 3b (₹1L+) later as "full concierge" if demand is there

The ₹25K price point was optimised for approachability. But approachability without margin kills the business. ₹50K is still approachable and doesn't bleed you on expert time.

## Q2 — Are people getting value from ₹499? Should we drop to ₹299?

**Short answer: Keep ₹499. Don't drop.**

### Why ₹499 works

- **Real value delivered.** Structured draft, CDSCO mapping, pathway, timeline. This is worth ₹25K–1L at consultants. ₹499 is a 50x discount, not a 10x discount. Founders know this.
- **Serious-buyer filter.** ₹499 filters out pure tire-kickers. ₹299 invites them in.
- **Unit economics.** API (Claude Opus on draft pack generation) + PDF + email + infra ≈ ₹100 cost. ₹499 → ₹400 margin. ₹299 → ₹200 margin. The difference compounds at scale.
- **Upsell anchor.** ₹499 → ₹50K concierge is an 100x jump. ₹299 → ₹50K is 170x. The mental gap to the concierge tier is smaller when Tier 1 starts higher.

### When to consider dropping

Only if one of these happens:
- Week-1 conversion from free card → ₹499 is **under 10%**: test ₹299
- Customers consistently say "too expensive": test ₹299
- You see high cart abandonment at checkout: test ₹299

### Alternative: raise to ₹999 with anchoring

There's a case for *raising* to ₹999 with copy like *"Typical regulatory consultants charge ₹50K–1L for this. ClearPath: ₹999."* Anchors value higher. But this only works if the draft pack quality is consistently strong — first few weeks of outputs need to be hand-reviewed.

**My recommendation: Ship at ₹499. Test ₹299 only if conversion is under 10%. Consider ₹999 anchoring once you have 10+ customers with positive feedback.**

### Landscape

Roughly **7,537 healthcare startups** are in the DPIIT "social impact" segment. Of these, a meaningful fraction are non-profit or Section-8 companies. Indian examples:

- **Khushi Baby** (already in our calibration set) — Section-8, NCD screening at public health scale
- **Doctors For You** — disaster medical response + clinical services
- **PATH India** — global health non-profit with tech tools
- **Jhpiego India** — maternal/child health tech
- **Pratham Health** — ancillary to Pratham Education
- **ARMMAN** — maternal health, award-winning tech
- **Noora Health** — caregiver education
- **Swasth Alliance** — ABDM-adjacent non-profit
- **Wadhwani AI** — AI for public good, including health
- **eGovernments Foundation** — public health tech

### Pricing implications

Non-profits **do face the same regulatory requirements** (DPDP, ABDM for gov hospital work, ICMR for validation, NABH for deployment). But they can't pay ₹25K–50K out of operational budget. Two options:

1. **Discounted tier for registered non-profits.** 50% off Tier 2, 75% off Tier 3. Requires Section-8/80G verification. Small revenue hit but credibility/distribution gain.
2. **Foundation-sponsored access.** Partner with Wadhwani Foundation, Tata Trusts, BIRAC, or CCAMP to sponsor non-profit access to ClearPath. Foundation pays in bulk; non-profits use for free. This is how PATH-style orgs already buy software.

**Not a direct revenue segment, but a brand/distribution channel.** A quote from "Khushi Baby's head of tech: 'ClearPath cut our NCD screening classification work from weeks to hours'" does more for ClearPath's credibility than most paid customer testimonials.

### The B2B-to-law-firms play (real opportunity)

**Law firms are customers, not competitors.**

A regulatory/life-sciences law firm uses ClearPath as:
1. **Junior-associate productivity tool** — 10 hours of research compressed to 30 minutes
2. **Client-intake qualification** — "Run ClearPath first, then we'll quote" prevents wasted engagement scoping
3. **First-pass regulatory analysis** — white-labelled for their client deliverables
4. **Continuous training** — associates learn Indian reg landscape faster by using the tool

### Why India-first is defensible but not a ceiling

India's regulatory frameworks (DPDP Act 2023, CDSCO SaMD 2025 draft, ICMR AI Guidelines 2023, SAHI strategy) are being **watched and cited** as references by emerging markets. Three reasons ClearPath's India-first positioning unlocks global:

1. **Indian regulatory work is a hard exam.** 9 overlapping regulations, fragmented bodies, fast-changing. If we can productise this cleanly, any other market is easier.
2. **Indian digital health talent and product sensibility.** Low-cost engineering + founder-native UX (not enterprise-consulting UX) is a hard combo to replicate.
3. **English-first regulatory documents.** All CDSCO, FDA, MHRA, EU MDR, TGA regulations are English. Same team can serve 5+ markets without language localisation.

### Roadmap

- **2026:** India-first (MVP through ₹10 Cr ARR)
- **2027:** Add CE Mark + FDA + MHRA pathways (₹10 → ₹100 Cr ARR). Begin B2B licensing to Indian law firms; pilot one US firm.
- **2028+:** Global South benchmark (Indonesia, Brazil, SA). Become the "Stripe of health regulation" for developing markets.
