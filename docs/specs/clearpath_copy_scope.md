```
Q: Does my product need CDSCO approval?
A: It depends on what your product does, not what you call it. 
CDSCO's Medical Device Rules 2017 and the Oct 2025 SaMD draft 
cover any software that influences clinical decisions. ClearPath 
assesses this in 5 minutes — for free.

Q: How is this different from hiring a regulatory consultant?
A: A consultant costs ₹50K–3L and takes 6–12 weeks to give you a 
classification opinion. ClearPath gives you the same initial 
classification in 5 minutes, free. For expert review + submission 
support, our Stage 3 Concierge (₹50K) adds a named expert. 
Consultants are still right for complex clinical trial design 
and long-term retainer work — we replace the first 4 weeks of 
discovery calls, not the whole relationship.

Q: What happens after I pay ₹499?
A: Within ~10 minutes, you get an email with (a) a ClearPath-branded 
Draft Pack PDF with drafted content for each CDSCO submission 
section, (b) the relevant blank CDSCO forms as downloadable PDFs, 
and (c) a submission guide showing which form goes to which authority 
in which order.

Q: What's in the ₹50K Submission Concierge?
A: A named Indian regulatory expert reviews your draft, validates 
classification, checks your QMS alignment with ISO 13485/IEC 62304, 
reviews your clinical validation plan, and helps refine your 
submission. Includes 1 iteration within 2 weeks of delivery. 
Currently waitlist-only for the launch cohort.

Q: Is my data safe?
A: Your submission data is used only to generate your Readiness 
Card and Draft Pack. We use Supabase (encrypted at rest) for storage 
and delete your data after 90 days unless you explicitly request 
retention. We are aligned with India's DPDP Act 2023. See our 
Privacy Policy for details.

Q: Will my uploaded documents stay private?
A: Yes. Any PDFs you upload during intake are stored encrypted, 
accessible only via signed URLs, and deleted after 90 days. We 
never share uploaded documents with third parties. See our 
Privacy Policy.

Q: Do you file the application for me?
A: No. ClearPath produces content and clarity. The actual CDSCO 
filing is done by you, your consultant, or through our Stage 3 
Concierge (expert-reviewed draft, you file). We are not authorised 
to file on your behalf.

Q: What if my product isn't a medical device at all?
A: That's a valid outcome — about 40% of products we analyse don't 
require CDSCO. The free card will tell you clearly if you're in 
the "wellness carve-out" zone (fitness, lifestyle, non-diagnostic) 
and what other regulations apply (usually DPDP for data, sometimes 
NABH for hospital-adjacent products).

Q: How accurate are your classifications?
A: We use "likely," not "definitely" — final classification always 
rests with CDSCO. Our engine is calibrated against 15+ real Indian 
products with known classifications. For regulatory-grade certainty, 
use our Stage 3 Concierge where a named expert validates.

Q: Can I get a refund?
A: ₹499 Draft Pack: full refund within 24 hours if the content doesn't 
match your product (we'll ask you to describe the mismatch). ₹50K 
Concierge: full refund if we can't assign an expert within 72 hours; 
otherwise prorated based on review stage.

Q: Who's behind ClearPath?
A: Built by Raunaq Pradhan — ABDM Champion Mentor, SIIP Fellow, 
with 10 years in Indian digital health. See the "Who built this" 
section above.
```

## 4.2 Question 1 — Clinical state

```
Question:
What clinical state does your product address?

Helper:
This determines how critical any error would be.

Options:
○ Critical / life-threatening
  Conditions where delay or error could result in death or severe harm.
  E.g. cardiac arrest, stroke, sepsis.

○ Serious / chronic
  Conditions requiring timely intervention but not immediately 
  life-threatening. E.g. diabetes, hypertension, cancer screening.

○ Non-serious / preventive
  General wellness, prevention, low-risk conditions. 
  E.g. fitness tracking, sleep monitoring.

○ Not sure / varies
  Multiple use cases with different clinical states.
```

## 4.3 Question 2 — Information significance

```
Question:
How much does your product influence clinical decisions?

Helper:
"Inform" = displays data. "Drive" = suggests action. 
"Diagnose/treat" = makes or executes clinical decisions.

Options:
○ Informs — displays information only
  Shows data or trends. Clinician interprets and decides.

○ Drives — flags or suggests
  Highlights abnormalities or suggests next steps. Clinician 
  reviews and acts.

○ Diagnoses or treats — makes clinical decisions
  Outputs a diagnosis, treatment recommendation, or automated action.
```
