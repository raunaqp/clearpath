Slide 6 · Reasoning architecture

Regulatory reasoning compression

~44 structured fields drive a graph of dozens of forms and documents. Rules + ontology + workflow engine — deterministic reasoning over CDSCO rule structure, not generative AI invention.

Founder input
Intake (7 fields) · Tier A (7 questions) · Tier B (6+2 questions) · structured pitch-deck extraction (~22 fields).
e.g. intended_use, clinical_state, decision_influence, ai_ml, data_sensitivity
▾
Ontology mapping
Inputs mapped to MDR-2017 concepts: device class, predicate status, conditional triggers (sterile, patient-contact, drug, software, AI/ML, veterinary).
e.g. decision_influence=drives + clinical_state=serious → SaMD Class B
▾
Workflow engine
Rules determine applicable form sequence, document checklist, conditional NOCs, and inter-form dependencies (e.g., MD-27 before MD-7).
e.g. has_predicate=no → MD-26 → MD-27 prerequisite for MD-7
▾
Document graph
DMF, PMF, QMS, regulatory NOCs cross-referenced. ~60-80 distinct documents covered across persona set.
e.g. sterile=yes → DMF §8.14 sterilisation validation required
▾
Workflow-assisted drafting
Per-section scaffold with explicit uncertainty flags. Founder-editable. Auditable provenance back to input fields. Expert review remains in the loop.
e.g. Class C MD-7 → 12-section submission scaffold with conditional sub-blocks
Branching decisions encoded in rules
A small slice of the deterministic decision tree. The same shape applies to dozens of other branches (sterile / patient-contact / drug content / veterinary / no-predicate).

Slide 8 · Ecosystem positioning

Why this matters for translational medtech ecosystems

The recurring pattern across BIG / TBI / incubator cohorts: technical depth is high; regulatory sequencing is low. ClearPath sits between proof-of-concept and consultant engagement.

1
Founders discover regulation too late. Device-class implications are often surfaced only after first investor diligence or first hospital pilot — by which point design and clinical-evidence choices are locked.
2
TRL ladder and regulatory readiness are disconnected. Technical TRL frameworks rarely surface MDR-2017 prerequisites at each stage; teams hit regulation as a wall rather than a parallel track.
3
Teams may initiate MD-22 clinical investigation discussions before obtaining MD-13 test manufacturing permissions. The sequencing dependency between MD-12/13 (test licence to manufacture trial devices) and MD-22/23 (CI permission) is a recurring source of rework.
4
AI/ML SaMD founders often underestimate ACP / change-management documentation requirements. The Oct 2025 draft guidance frames Algorithm Change Protocol as a five-component file. Teams shipping adaptive models discover this late.
5
Consultant access doesn't scale to every cohort member. Specialist regulatory consultants are gated by budget and time; programme-level support needs structured pre-consultant prep that is easier to triage.

Slide 10 · Transparency posture

UNCERTAIN flags — transparent by design

Where CDSCO rule text is silent, ambiguous, or in active draft, ClearPath surfaces uncertainty rather than fabricates confidence. The internal reference document carries 85 such flags; the most consequential are below.

IVD Master File appendix conflict
IVD FAQ §102 places contents in Appendix II of Fourth Schedule; multiple republished checklists reference Appendix III. Cascades through every IVD persona — needs authoritative ruling.
Algorithm Change Protocol — bridge regime
Oct 2025 SaMD Draft introduced ACP framework. Stakeholder comments closed Nov 2025; finalisation pending. What posture should an AI/ML SaMD shipping in 2026 take?
CTRI registration for medical device CI
Mandatory for pharma trials per NDCT 2019. MDR-2017 Seventh Schedule references protocol registration; whether CTRI specifically is required for medical device CI needs confirmation.
SEC composition and convening
Subject Expert Committee is referenced across novel-device, classification-dispute, and CI-waiver paths. Composition rules and scheduling cadence are not publicly codified.
Combination product PMOA determination
No published CDSCO procedure equivalent to FDA's Office of Combination Products determination. Borderline cases go to SEC — but the upstream decision rule is informal.
